The Cat Breed Gallery

We are finishing our PurrPets breed guide with the rarest, most exotic felines. These breeds often look like mini-wildcats or have unique textures!

The Cat Breed Gallery

Breed Name Visual Example The "Wild" Fact
Burmilla A rare cross between a Burmese and a Chinchilla Persian.
Snowshoe Pointed cats with white "boots" on all four paws.
Khao Manee The "Diamond Eye" cat—often has one blue eye and one gold eye.
Sokoke A natural breed from Kenya with a "wood-grain" coat pattern.
Havana Brown The only cat with a truly chocolate-brown coat and whiskers!
Australian Mist Australia's first home-grown breed; known for being very laid back.
Maine Coon The "Gentle Giant" with tufted ears and a huge, bushy tail.
Persian Cat Known for their long, luxurious coats and "sweet" flat faces.
Abyssinian One of the oldest breeds; highly active and very intelligent.
Egyptian Mau The fastest domestic cat; capable of running up to 30 mph!
Ragamuffin Like a Ragdoll, but even bigger and fluffier. Very mellow!
Himalayan A cross between a Persian and a Siamese; beautiful blue eyes.
Selkirk Rex The "Cat in Sheep's Clothing" with thick, curly, wooly fur.
Somali A long-haired version of the Abyssinian with a fox-like tail.
Singapura The world's smallest domestic cat breed. Full-grown adults stay tiny!
Korat A silver-blue cat from Thailand; they have heart-shaped faces.
Tonkinese A perfect blend of Siamese and Burmese; very playful and affectionate.
Turkish Van The "Swimming Cat." They have a rare coat that loves the water.

Highlight: The Turkish Van

The Turkish Van is known as the "Swimming Cat." Unlike most cats who hate water, this breed has a unique cashmere-like texture to its fur that is water-resistant, and they actually enjoy taking a dip in the bathtub or pool!
