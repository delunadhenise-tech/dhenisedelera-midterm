Dog Health & Preventative Care

At PurrPets, we believe that a proactive approach to health is the best way to ensure your dog lives a long, happy life. Regular vet check-ups and staying up-to-date on shots are non-negotiable.


An annual exam can catch health issues before they become serious.

Essential Vaccination Schedule

Vaccines protect your dog from highly contagious and sometimes fatal diseases. Here are the core vaccines every dog needs:

Vaccine Protects Against Frequency
Rabies Fatal viral disease (required by law in most places) Every 1 to 3 years
DHPP Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, and Parainfluenza Boosters every 3 years
Bordetella Kennel Cough (highly contagious in social settings) Every 6 to 12 months
Leptospirosis Bacterial infection from wildlife/water Annually (if at risk)

Signs Your Dog Needs a Vet

Dogs are experts at hiding pain. Watch for these red flags:

  • Changes in Appetite: Refusing food for more than 24 hours.
  • Lethargy: Unusual tiredness or lack of interest in playing.
  • Digestive Issues: Frequent vomiting or diarrhea.
  • Skin Problems: Constant scratching, red patches, or hair loss.

Parasite Prevention

Don't forget the "Big Three" of parasites: Fleas, Ticks, and Heartworms. Most of these can be prevented with a simple monthly pill or topical treatment. Heartworm, in particular, is transmitted by mosquitoes and can be fatal if left untreated.
