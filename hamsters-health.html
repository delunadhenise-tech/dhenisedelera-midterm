Hamster Health & Wellness

A healthy hamster is an active hamster! At PurrPets, we recommend a "Full Body Check" every week while you are bonding with your pet.


Bright eyes and a twitching nose are signs of a happy hammy!

The Weekly Health Checklist

Check Area Healthy Signs Warning Signs
Coat & Skin Smooth fur, no flakes. Bald spots, redness, or scratching.
Eyes & Nose Clear and dry. Cloudiness, discharge, or sneezing.
Bottom Area Clean and dry. Wetness or staining (Warning: Wet Tail!).
Teeth Yellow/Orange and straight. Overgrown teeth or drooling.

The Emergency: Wet Tail

Wet Tail is a very serious bacterial infection caused by stress or poor hygiene. It causes severe diarrhea and can be fatal within 48 hours if not treated by a vet. If your hamster's tail area looks damp or soiled, seek medical help immediately.

The Sand Bath Secret: Hamsters should never be bathed in water. To keep their fur clean and oil-free, provide a dish of sterilized "Chinchilla Sand" (not dust!). They will roll in it to groom themselves!

Hibernation vs. Torpor

If your room gets too cold, your hamster might enter a state called Torpor. They will look lifeless and feel cold. At PurrPets, we remind owners to keep hamster rooms between 18°C and 24°C. If they enter torpor, warm them up slowly with your body heat!
